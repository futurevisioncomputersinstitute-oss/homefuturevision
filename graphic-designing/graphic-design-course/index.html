Course Structure

What You'll Learn

01
Weeks 1–8

Design Fundamentals

Gestalt principles, grid systems, typography, colour theory, visual hierarchy. Photoshop and Illustrator from the beginning.

02
Weeks 9–18

Print Design

Brochures, posters, editorial layouts, book covers, annual reports, packaging labels. InDesign mastery and CMYK print production.

03
Weeks 19–28

Brand Identity

Logo systems, brand guidelines, stationery, brand applications across digital and print, client presentation and identity proposals.

04
Weeks 29–38

Digital & Motion Design

UI basics in Figma, social media design systems, motion graphics in After Effects, video title design and animated brand elements.

05
Weeks 39–44

Full portfolio curation, personal brand website, freelance pricing and client management, final mentored presentation and industry connections.

Common Questions

FAQ

Is this suitable for someone with zero experience?
Yes. This is the flagship course and starts from complete basics. Many of our students have never opened Photoshop before.
How is this different from the Digital Design course?
The Digital Design course is focused on marketing outputs. This course spans the full profession — print, brand, motion and digital all in depth.
Do I need my own computer?
Yes. A laptop with at least 8GB RAM is recommended. Adobe Creative Cloud and Figma (free plan) are the primary tools.
What does the final portfolio include?
12–18 projects spanning print, brand identity, digital and motion — a full-breadth portfolio that demonstrates professional range.
Are classes in English or Gujarati?
Classes are taught in a mix of English and Gujarati/Hindi to ensure every student grasps concepts fully.
