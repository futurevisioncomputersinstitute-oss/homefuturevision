Course Curriculum

5 Months. 5 Phases.

01

UX Research & Strategy

Month 1 · FigJam · Notion AI · NotebookLM · Perplexity · ChatGPT · Claude · Gemini

Great design starts with understanding people. You’ll learn the full UX research toolkit — user interviews, surveys, competitor analysis and synthesis — and use AI to accelerate every step. NotebookLM and Perplexity become your research assistants. ChatGPT, Claude and Gemini help you analyse data, write research reports and generate user scenarios at speed. FigJam is where all research gets mapped visually.

  • ✓User research methods: interviews, surveys, contextual inquiry
  • ✓FigJam: collaborative empathy maps, affinity diagrams, user journey maps
  • ✓AI research: NotebookLM for insight extraction, Perplexity for competitive analysis
  • ✓ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini: personas, user stories, problem statement writing
  • ✓Notion AI: research documentation, project management for UX sprints
  • ✓Deliverables: persona deck, user journey map, research report
02

Wireframing & Information Architecture

Month 2 · Figma · FigJam · Uizard · Galileo AI · Relume AI

Turn research into structure. You’ll learn information architecture — how to organise content so users find what they need — then build low and mid-fidelity wireframes in Figma. AI tools like Uizard, Galileo AI and Relume AI let you generate initial wireframe directions from text prompts, which you then evaluate, critique and refine. This phase teaches you to think before you design.

  • ✓Information architecture: sitemaps, card sorting, user flows, navigation systems
  • ✓Figma: low-fi and mid-fi wireframes, frames, layout grids, auto-layout
  • ✓Uizard & Galileo AI: prompt-to-wireframe, sketch-to-UI conversion
  • ✓Relume AI: sitemap and wireframe generation for web projects
  • ✓Deliverables: full app sitemap, annotated wireframe set (20+ screens)
03

High-Fidelity UI Design

Month 3 · Figma · Figma AI · Adobe Photoshop · Photoshop AI · Adobe Firefly · Canva · Canva AI

This is where the product comes to life visually. You’ll build pixel-perfect screens, design systems and component libraries in Figma. Figma AI accelerates repetitive tasks and helps generate design variants. Photoshop and Firefly create custom imagery. Canva fills rapid marketing and onboarding asset needs. You’ll design for mobile, tablet and desktop with full responsive logic.

  • ✓Figma: high-fidelity UI, design systems, component libraries, variables, tokens
  • ✓Figma AI: auto-rename layers, design suggestions, content generation
  • ✓Photoshop + Photoshop AI: custom hero images, background removal, generative expand
  • ✓Adobe Firefly: text-to-image, generative fill for UI illustration assets
  • ✓Canva + Canva AI: onboarding illustrations, app store screenshots, marketing screens
  • ✓Deliverables: complete UI design system + 40+ high-fidelity screens
04

Prototyping, No-Code & AI Dev Tools

Month 4 · Figma · Framer · Webflow · Lovable · v0 · Bolt.new

Modern UX designers are expected to go beyond static screens. You’ll build interactive Figma prototypes, then use Framer and Webflow to create live websites from your designs — no code needed. The real edge in this phase is AI dev tools: Lovable, v0 and Bolt.new let you generate full working interfaces from prompts, which you design, direct and refine. You become the designer who can ship.

  • ✓Figma: advanced prototyping, micro-interactions, smart animate, usability testing
  • ✓Framer: component-based web design, animations, CMS, publish to live URL
  • ✓Webflow: visual web builder, interactions, CMS collections, responsive layout
  • ✓Lovable, v0 & Bolt.new: AI-generated UI code, prompt-to-product workflows
  • ✓Deliverables: interactive prototype, live Framer or Webflow site, AI-built app screen
05

Portfolio, Case Studies & Career Launch

Month 5 · All Tools · Notion AI · Claude · ChatGPT

Your UX portfolio is your interview. This phase is entirely dedicated to curating, writing and presenting 3–5 complete UX case studies that walk hiring managers through your research-to-design process. Claude and ChatGPT sharpen your case study writing. Notion AI organises your process documentation. You receive live feedback from mentors in mock portfolio reviews and interview simulations.

  • ✓3–5 complete UX case studies: problem, research, wireframes, UI, outcome
  • ✓Portfolio website built in Framer or Webflow
  • ✓Claude & ChatGPT: case study copy, cover letter writing, LinkedIn bio
  • ✓Mock interviews, portfolio presentations, mentor feedback sessions
  • ✓Placement guidance: resume, LinkedIn, job application strategy

Common Questions

FAQs

Do I need coding skills?

No. Design skills are the focus. Basic HTML/CSS awareness is a bonus but not a requirement. You’ll leave with Figma and Webflow skills that cover most digital design workflows.

Is 5 months enough to get job-ready?

Yes. The curriculum is intensive and project-led. You graduate with 4–6 full UX case studies — more than most applicants at entry level.

What’s the difference between UX and UI?

UX covers research, structure and logic — how a product works. UI covers visual design and interaction — how it looks and feels. This course teaches both in depth.

Will I work on real briefs?

Yes. Every phase uses industry-style briefs across fintech, healthcare, e-commerce and SaaS. Your portfolio will show real-world problem-solving, not just exercises.

Is placement support provided?

Portfolio reviews, interview preparation and placement guidance are all included. Our support is structured and active throughout and after the programme.
