Course Curriculum

What You’ll Learn

01

UX Foundations

Weeks 1–5

User research methods, personas, empathy mapping, user journey mapping, competitor analysis and problem framing. Build the thinking skills that underpin great product design.

02

Wireframing & Prototyping

Weeks 6–10

Information architecture, low-fidelity wireframes, Figma from scratch, clickable prototypes and design system basics. Every concept becomes interactive.

03

Visual UI Design

Weeks 11–16

High-fidelity UI screens, typography and colour for digital contexts, icon systems, responsive design, micro-interactions and advanced Figma techniques.

04

Testing & Portfolio

Weeks 17–20

Usability testing, design iteration, Webflow basics, UX case study writing, portfolio presentation and mock client pitches with mentor critique.

Common Questions

FAQs

Do I need coding skills?

No. Design skills are the focus. Basic HTML/CSS awareness is a bonus but not a requirement. You’ll leave with Figma and Webflow skills that cover most digital design workflows.

Is 5 months enough to get job-ready?

Yes. The curriculum is intensive and project-led. You graduate with 4–6 full UX case studies — more than most applicants at entry level.

What’s the difference between UX and UI?

UX covers research, structure and logic — how a product works. UI covers visual design and interaction — how it looks and feels. This course teaches both in depth.

Will I work on real briefs?

Yes. Every phase uses industry-style briefs across fintech, healthcare, e-commerce and SaaS. Your portfolio will show real-world problem-solving, not just exercises.

Is placement support provided?

Portfolio reviews, interview preparation and placement guidance are all included. Our support is structured and active throughout and after the programme.
